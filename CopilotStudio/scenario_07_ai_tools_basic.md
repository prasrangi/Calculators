# Scenario 7: AI Tools — Basic

## What It Is

Basic AI tools cover simple text processing tasks: text recognition, sentiment analysis, language detection, key phrase extraction, and prediction (both scheduled and real-time).

## When It's Used

- Analyzing customer sentiment in support tickets before routing
- Detecting the language of incoming messages to route to correct agent
- Extracting key phrases for ticket categorization or tagging
- Running prediction models on incoming data

## Credit Consumption

**1 Copilot Credit per 10 responses** (0.1 credits per response)

## Licensed vs. Unlicensed User Comparison

| User Type | Credits Per 10 Responses | Monthly Cost (10,000 responses) |
|-----------|--------------------------|--------------------------------|
| M365 Copilot Licensed User (B2E, internal channel) | 0 (included) | $0 |
| Unlicensed User | 1 credit per 10 responses | $10 |
| External User | 1 credit per 10 responses | $10 |

## Cost Comparison: PAYG vs. Capacity Pack

**Example — Sentiment Analysis Agent:** Analyzes 30,000 customer messages/month for sentiment.

- Credits = (30,000 ÷ 10) × 1 = 3,000 credits/month

| Billing Model | Calculation | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost |
|---------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Pay-As-You-Go | 3,000 × $0.01 | $30 | $360 |
| 1 Capacity Pack | $200 (22,000 credits wasted) | $200 | $2,400 |

**Verdict:** Basic AI tools are very cheap. Unless combined with other features consuming credits, PAYG is almost always the better option for basic-only workloads.
